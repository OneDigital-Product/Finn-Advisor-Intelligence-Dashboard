# OneDigital Advisor Command Center

### The intelligent platform built for how wealth advisors actually work.

---

## Your Complete Book of Business, One Screen Away

The Advisor Command Center brings together everything you need to serve your clients, prepare for meetings, stay compliant, and grow your practice. Powered by AI and connected to the systems you already use, it replaces scattered tools and manual workflows with a single, purpose-built experience.

---

## My Day Dashboard

Start every morning with a clear picture of what matters most. The dashboard is your command center -- everything you need to plan your day, respond to what's urgent, and keep your practice running smoothly.

### Book Snapshot

Four summary cards at the top of the screen give you an instant read on your practice:

- **Total AUM** -- Your combined assets under management across all households, with month-to-date change tracking so you can see momentum at a glance.
- **Net Flows** -- Year-to-date and month-to-date net flow figures showing how capital is moving in and out of your book.
- **Revenue YTD** -- Estimated year-to-date revenue calculated from your AUM so you can track against targets.
- **Clients** -- Total client count with a mini-breakdown by segment (A, B, C, D) so you can see your book composition without navigating away.

### Today's Schedule

Your meetings for the day are laid out with start and end times, meeting title, type (Initial Consultation, Quarterly Review, etc.), location, and a direct link to the client's profile. Each meeting also shows the client's AUM and segment tier so you know the context before you walk in.

Every meeting has a one-click **Prep** button. Clicking it triggers the AI to generate a full preparation brief tailored to that specific client and meeting -- no switching between systems, no manual research. The brief appears immediately and covers everything you need to walk into the conversation ready.

### Action Queue

Your top 10 pending tasks, sorted by urgency. Each task shows the title, associated client name, due date, and priority level. Priorities are color-coded -- high-priority items in red, medium in amber, low in grey -- so you can scan quickly and decide what needs attention first.

### Alerts

Real-time notifications for events that need your attention, organized by severity:

- **Critical alerts** (red) flag items like large withdrawal requests or significant account changes that require immediate action.
- **Warning alerts** (amber) surface things like required minimum distributions not yet taken or compliance deadlines approaching.
- **Informational alerts** provide context on client milestones, market events, or system updates.

Each alert can be dismissed with a single click. Dismissed alerts are removed from the active view so your dashboard stays clean and focused on what still needs attention.

### Upcoming Meetings

Beyond today's schedule, a sidebar widget shows your next five upcoming meetings across any date, so you always have visibility into what's coming and can prepare in advance.

## Client 360 View

Everything you know about a client, organized and actionable. The client detail page is the deepest part of the platform, organized into tabbed sections so you can quickly navigate to exactly what you need.

### Contact & Profile

Full client profile with email, phone, mailing address, date of birth, and personal interests. A persistent advisor notes field lets you record observations, preferences, or context that matters for the relationship -- notes are saved automatically and visible every time you open the client's record.

Data source indicators show where each piece of information originates (for example, a Salesforce badge on contact details), so you always know whether you're looking at CRM data, portfolio data, or something else.

### Accounts & Holdings

All client accounts listed with account type, custodian, current balance, and tax status (taxable, tax-deferred, tax-exempt). Accounts are pulled from your portfolio management system and displayed in a clean table format.

The **live holdings table** goes deeper. Every individual position shows:

- **Security name and ticker symbol**
- **Current shares held and cost basis**
- **Real-time market price** updated from live market data feeds
- **Day change** (dollar and percentage) so you can see what's moving
- **Unrealized gain/loss** calculated from cost basis vs. current market price
- **Account attribution** showing which account holds each position

This means you can walk into a client meeting and speak to exactly what's happening in their portfolio right now -- not as of last night's batch update.

### Portfolio Analytics

Visual breakdowns of the client's financial position:

- **Asset Allocation** -- A donut chart showing how the portfolio is distributed across asset classes (US Equity, International Equity, Fixed Income, Cash, Alternatives, etc.) with dollar amounts for each slice. This makes it easy to spot over-concentrations or allocation drift during a conversation.
- **Performance vs. Benchmark** -- A bar chart comparing portfolio returns against benchmarks over multiple time periods (1 month, 3 months, YTD, 1 year, 3 years). Each period shows the return percentage, the benchmark percentage, and the difference -- positive alpha is highlighted in green, underperformance in red.
- **Portfolio News** -- A real-time news feed that automatically filters by the specific tickers held in the client's portfolio. Instead of scanning general market news, you see only the stories relevant to what this client owns. Each article shows the headline, source, publication time, and a link to the full story.

### Alternative Assets

A dedicated section for tracking assets that don't live in traditional brokerage accounts. Each alternative asset entry captures:

- **Asset name and type** (real estate, private equity, private debt, collectibles, etc.)
- **Estimated current value and original cost basis**
- **Acquisition date**
- **Location** (for real estate) or fund/manager details
- **Notes** for any context that doesn't fit a standard field

This gives you a complete picture of the client's net worth, not just the managed portfolio.

### Household View

See the full family picture with relationships clearly mapped. Each household member shows their name and relationship to the primary client (spouse, child, parent, etc.). This is essential for estate planning conversations, beneficiary reviews, and understanding the family dynamics that drive financial decisions.

### Life Events Timeline

A chronological list of significant events -- birthdays, wedding anniversaries, retirement target dates, births of children or grandchildren, graduations, home purchases, and more. Life events are the natural conversation starters and planning triggers. Having them visible on the client profile means you never miss an opportunity to demonstrate that you know and care about the client's life beyond their portfolio.

### Team Collaboration

Assign associates to each client's service team. Roles include Support, Analyst, Paraplanner, and other designations. Team members appear as avatars in the client header, and you can add or remove members with a single click. Hovering over a team member's avatar reveals a remove button, making it easy to adjust the team as roles change.

## AI-Powered Meeting Intelligence

The meeting workflow is where AI delivers the most immediate time savings. Every stage of a client meeting -- preparation, the conversation itself, and follow-up -- is enhanced.

### Meeting Prep Briefs

Before every meeting, click "Prep" to generate a comprehensive preparation document. The AI pulls together all relevant client data and produces a brief that includes:

- **Suggested agenda items** based on the client's current financial situation, outstanding action items, and upcoming life events
- **Portfolio performance summary** with returns vs. benchmarks across multiple time periods
- **Outstanding tasks** and follow-up items from prior meetings so you know what was promised
- **Relevant life events** and planning milestones that might come up in conversation
- **Compliance checklist** items that need attention or signatures

The prep brief is generated in seconds and is specific to the individual client -- not a generic template. The AI considers the client's holdings, account types, recent activity, compliance status, and personal circumstances to produce something you can actually use.

### Transcript Analysis

After a meeting, upload the transcript from Zoom, Microsoft Teams, or any other recording platform. The system accepts plain text, VTT subtitle files, or direct text paste. A sample transcript is available for testing.

Once uploaded, the AI analyzes the full conversation and produces:

- **A structured meeting summary** covering what was discussed in detail
- **Action items** extracted from the conversation, each assigned to either the advisor or the client, with a priority level (high, medium, low) and suggested due date
- **Key topics** identified from the discussion (Portfolio Review, Estate Planning, Tax Strategy, etc.)
- **Client sentiment** assessment -- positive, neutral, or concerned -- giving you a read on how the client is feeling about their situation
- **Compliance observations** flagging anything in the conversation that has regulatory implications

The meeting record is created automatically, action items are added to your task list, and everything is linked to the client's profile. What used to take 20-30 minutes of post-meeting note-taking happens in seconds.

### Meeting Summaries

For meetings where you have notes but not a full transcript, the summary generator takes your notes and produces a polished, comprehensive summary. It captures key decisions, client concerns, topics discussed, and recommended next steps. The AI also suggests follow-up tasks based on the discussion content, complete with suggested owners and due dates.

### Talking Points

Before a meeting, generate 5-8 personalized talking points that reference the client's actual holdings, recent portfolio performance, and current life situation. These aren't generic prompts -- they mention specific securities, specific dollar amounts, and specific life events so you can walk in with conversation starters that demonstrate deep knowledge of the client's world.

### Follow-Up Emails

After a meeting, generate a professional follow-up email that summarizes the discussion, confirms next steps, and maintains the warm, personal tone your clients expect. The email references what was actually discussed -- not boilerplate -- and is ready to review, edit if needed, and send.

## AI Financial Assessment

Run comprehensive, AI-powered diagnostic analyses of any client's complete financial position.

### How It Works

When you click "Run Assessment," the platform gathers all available data for the client -- accounts, holdings, performance history, household information, compliance status, outstanding tasks, documents on file, and recent activity. This data package is sent to the AI along with a structured analysis prompt, and the result is a detailed financial health report.

### Configurable Assessments

Your firm can create multiple assessment configurations, each with a different analysis focus. For example, you might have one configuration for comprehensive annual reviews, another focused on retirement readiness, and a third for risk assessment. When you run an assessment, you choose which configuration to use from a dropdown -- so the analysis is tailored to the conversation you're having.

Each configuration has its own analysis prompt (controlling what the AI evaluates and how it writes the report) and its own HTML template (controlling the visual format of the output). Administrators create and manage these configurations, and advisors choose from the active ones when running assessments.

### What the Report Covers

A typical assessment evaluates:

- **Portfolio health** including allocation balance, sector exposure, and position sizing
- **Diversification rating** with specific observations about concentration risk
- **Performance trends** relative to appropriate benchmarks
- **Compliance status** and any outstanding regulatory items
- **Planning gaps** based on the client's stated goals and current trajectory
- **Specific recommendations** tied to the data

### Report History

Every assessment you run is saved with a timestamp and the configuration name used. You can click any historical entry to view that report again, compare how a client's situation has changed over time, or reference a previous analysis in a meeting.

### Export

One-click PDF export generates a branded, client-ready document. Every report includes a clear AI-generated disclaimer so there's no ambiguity about how the analysis was produced.

## Retirement Planning & Monte Carlo Simulation

Give clients confidence in their plan with data-driven projections they can see and understand.

### Scenario Builder

Create retirement scenarios by defining the key variables:

- **Current age and target retirement age** to set the planning horizon
- **Current portfolio value** as the starting point
- **Annual spending goal in retirement** in today's dollars
- **Annual contributions** during the working years
- **Expected return and volatility assumptions** to model market behavior
- **Inflation rate** to adjust future spending needs

You can create multiple scenarios side by side -- for example, "retire at 62" vs. "retire at 65" or "aggressive allocation" vs. "moderate allocation" -- so clients can see how different choices change the outcome.

### Life Event Integration

Layer in specific income and expense events that will occur at known points in the future:

- **Social Security start date and estimated benefit**
- **Pension income** starting at a specific age
- **College tuition** for children or grandchildren over a defined period
- **An expected inheritance** or property sale
- **Healthcare cost changes** at Medicare eligibility
- **Any other one-time or recurring financial event**

These events are integrated into the simulation so projections reflect the client's actual expected cash flows, not just a smooth spending line.

### Monte Carlo Engine

Each scenario runs **1,000 independent simulations**, each with randomly generated market returns based on the specified expected return and volatility. The simulation uses a Box-Muller transform for statistical accuracy and accounts for inflation, annual spending, contributions, and life events in every single run.

The result is a **probability-weighted success rate** -- the percentage of simulations where the portfolio lasted through the full planning horizon without running out of money.

### Projection Charts

Results are displayed as a fan chart showing the range of outcomes:

- **90th percentile** (optimistic scenario)
- **75th percentile**
- **50th percentile** (median outcome)
- **25th percentile**
- **10th percentile** (pessimistic scenario)

Clients can see not just the expected outcome but the range of possibilities, which makes for much more productive and realistic planning conversations.

### PDF Export

Generate a branded retirement projection report that packages the scenario inputs, success rate, projection chart, and life events into a clean document you can hand to the client or include in their planning file.

## Document Management & AI Parsing

Stop manual data entry. Let the AI read your documents and update client records.

### AI Document Extraction

Upload an account statement, tax return, financial plan, or any other client document. The AI reads the document and extracts structured data in three categories:

- **Profile updates** -- email addresses, phone numbers, dates of birth, and other personal information found in the document
- **Accounts** -- account numbers, types, custodians, and balances identified in the document
- **Holdings** -- individual security positions with ticker symbols, share counts, and values

Extracted data is presented in a review panel before anything is applied. You see exactly what the AI found, can accept or reject individual items, and then apply the updates to the client record with a click. Nothing changes without your review and approval.

### AI Document Classification

When a document is uploaded, the AI automatically identifies which compliance checklist item it satisfies. For example, uploading a driver's license image would be matched to "Government-Issued Photo ID" and uploading a signed advisory agreement would be matched to "Signed Advisory Agreement."

Each classification includes a **confidence score** and reasoning so you can verify the AI's judgment. The matched checklist item is updated automatically, saving the manual step of finding and checking off the right box.

### Document Checklist

Every client has a document checklist tracking what's required and what's been received. Items are organized by category (Identity & KYC, Financial Statements, Tax Documents, Legal Documents, etc.) and show:

- Whether the document has been received or is still outstanding
- The date it was received
- Any notes from the advisor

A visual progress indicator shows the completion percentage at a glance, so you can quickly see which clients need follow-up to complete their files.

### Document Vault

All uploaded documents are stored securely with metadata including:

- **Document name and type** (Statement, Tax Return, Legal Agreement, etc.)
- **Status** (signed, pending, draft, filed)
- **Upload date**
- **Download access** for retrieving any document on file

## Compliance Center

Stay ahead of regulatory requirements across your entire book of business.

### How Compliance Tracking Works

Every client has a set of compliance items that need to be maintained -- risk profile reviews, suitability reviews, IPS reviews, estate plan reviews, beneficiary updates, concentrated position documentation, and more. Each item has a status, a due date, and a completion history.

### Compliance Item Statuses

Items move through four statuses based on their due dates and completion state:

- **Current** -- the item is up to date and no action is needed
- **Expiring Soon** -- the item is approaching its due date and should be addressed
- **Overdue** -- the item has passed its due date and needs immediate attention
- **Pending** -- initial state, awaiting first completion

### Compliance Review Workflow

For items that require formal review, the platform supports a multi-step review process:

1. **Draft** -- the advisor prepares the review, selecting which compliance items to include
2. **Submitted** -- the review is sent to the compliance team for evaluation
3. **Under Review** -- a compliance officer has picked up the review
4. **Approved** -- the review is finalized, and linked compliance items are automatically updated to "current"
5. **Changes Requested** -- the reviewer sends it back to the advisor for corrections

Each review maintains a timeline of events so you can see exactly when it was created, submitted, reviewed, and approved.

### Health Score

The compliance health score is calculated as the percentage of items that are current out of total items. The score is displayed as a visual gauge on the client's compliance tab:

- **80% or higher** -- green, healthy
- **50-79%** -- amber, needs attention
- **Below 50%** -- red, requires immediate action

### Advisor Actions

From the compliance tab, advisors can:

- **Start a review** by selecting compliance items and creating a draft
- **Submit reviews** to the compliance team
- **Mark items as complete** directly when formal review isn't required
- **View the full history** of compliance events for any item or review

## Visual Workflow Designer

Build repeatable processes for your practice without writing a single line of code. The workflow designer is a full visual canvas for creating multi-step operational workflows.

### Canvas

The workflow canvas is a zoomable, pannable workspace where you design processes by placing and connecting steps. The canvas features a grid background for alignment, and you can zoom from 0.3x to 2x to work with workflows of any complexity. An auto-fit button centers and scales the entire workflow to fit your screen.

### Steps

Each step in a workflow is a card on the canvas with a title and description. Steps can be dragged to any position, and their layout is saved so the workflow looks the same every time you open it.

Steps support three output types that determine what happens after the step is completed:

- **Simple (no output)** -- the step is completed and the workflow moves to the next connected step. Used for tasks like "Review client file" or "Send welcome packet."
- **Free text response** -- the person executing the workflow types a response before proceeding. Used for steps like "Enter advisor notes" or "Document client goals."
- **Multiple choice** -- the step presents a set of predefined options, and the choice determines which branch of the workflow to follow next. Used for decision points like "Is the client a qualified purchaser? Yes / No" or "Risk tolerance: Conservative / Moderate / Aggressive."

### Connections

Steps are connected by dragging from a port on one step to another step. Connections are rendered as smooth curved lines so the flow is easy to follow visually. Clicking a connection removes it, and reconnecting is as simple as dragging again.

For multiple choice steps, each choice option gets its own connection port, so you can route different answers to different subsequent steps. This is how branching logic works -- a single decision point can fork the workflow into two or more parallel paths.

### Task Automation

Any step can be configured to automatically create a task when it's completed. The task automation settings include:

- **Task title** -- what the generated task will be called
- **Task type** -- Follow-up, Document Request, Compliance, etc.
- **Due date offset** -- how many days after step completion the task should be due

This means your workflows don't just guide the process -- they automatically create the follow-up items that keep things moving.

### Templates

Workflows are saved as reusable templates. Start from pre-built templates for common processes like client onboarding, annual review preparation, or financial planning engagements, then customize them for your practice. Templates can be duplicated and modified so you can maintain variations without starting from scratch.

## Administration

Everything your operations team needs to configure, monitor, and manage the platform.

### User Management

Create and manage advisor profiles with full name, title, email, and login credentials. The admin view shows each advisor's client count and total AUM so you can monitor capacity and distribution across the team. Advisor records can be edited at any time, and new advisors can be added through a simple form.

### Operational Reports

Four purpose-built reports, each with CSV export for offline analysis:

- **AUM Summary** -- Every client listed with their segment, total assets, and estimated annual revenue. Sortable and filterable to analyze your book from any angle.
- **Client Engagement** -- Contact frequency and engagement status (Active, Recent, Aging, At Risk) for every client. Shows the exact number of days since last contact so you can identify service gaps.
- **Compliance Status** -- Firm-wide compliance item tracking showing status distribution (Current, Expiring Soon, Overdue, Pending) across all clients. The single view that tells you whether the firm is meeting its regulatory obligations.
- **Document Checklist** -- Document collection completion rates by client, distinguishing between total documents and required documents. Helps operations teams follow up on missing paperwork systematically.

### AI Prompt Configuration

Fine-tune exactly how AI behaves across three key workflows using a dedicated prompt editor:

- **Meeting Prep** -- Configure the system prompt and user prompt that generate preparation briefs. Merge fields let you inject dynamic data (client name, holdings list, performance data, life events, etc.) so the AI has full context.
- **Meeting Summary** -- Configure how the AI generates post-meeting summaries and extracts action items. Adjust the tone, detail level, and focus areas to match your firm's documentation standards.
- **Document Classification** -- Configure how the AI categorizes uploaded documents against your compliance checklist. Adjust the classification logic and confidence thresholds to match your document types.

Each prompt configuration supports multiple versions. You can create alternatives, mark one as active, and switch between them without losing previous configurations.

### Assessment Configuration

Create and manage the financial assessment configurations that advisors use when running AI assessments:

- **Analysis prompts** control what the AI evaluates and how it structures its findings
- **HTML report templates** control the visual format of the generated report
- **Merge fields** (using `{{field.path}}` syntax) inject dynamic client data into the report template
- **Template preview** lets you see the visual output before deploying a configuration to advisors
- **Active/inactive status** controls which configurations appear in the advisor's dropdown

### System Settings

A real-time system health dashboard showing:

- **Database status** -- PostgreSQL connection health
- **API server status** -- Express server health
- **AI service status** -- OpenAI connection health, including whether the system is operating in full AI mode or fallback mode (regex-based analysis when AI is unavailable)
- **Integration status** for Salesforce CRM, Orion Portfolio, and OpenAI connections
- **Advisor capacity metrics** showing current client count vs. target capacity
- **API integration guide** with technical documentation for connecting live production data sources
